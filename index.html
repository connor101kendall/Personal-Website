EMG Controlled Prosthetic

SolidWorks, Arduino, Circuits, LTspice, 3D Printing

A medium-fidelity prototype for an EMG-controlled screw-driving prosthesis catering to lower limb amputees. The circuit was designed implementing advanced signal filtering on EMG signals through ICs, resistors, and capacitors. The planetary gear mechanism was tested with FEA techniques to ensure reliability. The prototype was designed in SolidWorks and 3D-printed in PLA.

04/2024

Postnatal Reliable Incubator State Machine(P.R.I.S.M)

FSM, Arduino, Digital Logic Design

A Mealy Finite State Machine (FSM) capable of managing critical parameters such as heart rate, respiration rate, and temperature, while integrating status indicators, heating, ventilation, and alarm functionalities. A detailed state diagram, transition table, and simplified boolean equations was used in the design phase, followed by validation through FALSTAD simulation software to ensure integration and optimal performance. The design was translated into a physical prototype by implementing the circuit on a breadboard using components like AND/OR gates and D flip flops

07/2025

Brake/Motor current limiting test PCB

KiCAD, PCB Design, PCB Assembly

A dual purpose test board designed to interface with the brakes and motors of a surgical robotic system to test the current limiting capabilities. The board consists of a high tolerance low resistance sensing resistor and a high wattage adjustable shunt resistor. The boards components were selected to allow drawing precise current slightly above the trip current for 4 unique brakes and one unique motor. The board was hand assembled using both surface mount and through-hole PCB mounting techniques. The schematic and layout of the board was created using KiCAD

07/2025

Mechanical Test Rig Platform

Onshape, DFM/DFA, Sensors, Regulatory

Multipurpose test rig platform designed to prove product requirements for a surgical robots FDA 510(k) submission. Test platform can interface with an ATI multi-axis sensor and matches attachment style of specific components from the surgical robotic system. Individual parts were designed in Onshape following DFM and DFA principles. Parts were machined in aluminum then assembled by hand and used in multiple test scenarios.
